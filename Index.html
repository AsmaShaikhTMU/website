About

15+ years at the intersection of public-sector strategy, data, and real projects.

My name is Asma Shaikh, and for over 15 years, I've had a unique vantage point: the intersection of public-sector strategy, data, and the real-world projects that bring it all to life. I don't see these as separate disciplines; I see them as three sides of a single coin.

My journey began with a hands-on understanding of buildings and infrastructure as a Building Science Professional. I learned what it takes to build and maintain a strong foundation. But I quickly realized that the best technical plans fall apart without a strong financial strategy and a clear path to execution.

This led me to a dual focus: translating complex financial data into actionable recommendations for senior leadership and re-engineering the very systems that support large-scale projects. At Toronto Metropolitan University, for example, I led an initiative to improve procurement efficiency by 20% and boosted cost tracking accuracy by 15%.

Today, armed with a PMP certification and a knack for finding the story in the data, my mission is to connect all these dots. I'm passionate about helping organizations like Metrolinx achieve their strategic goals by ensuring their capital plans are not just visions on a page, but well‑oiled machines built for success.
